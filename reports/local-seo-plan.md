# Local SEO Plan — north Brisbane / Moreton Bay

**Prepared:** 2026-07-20
**Status:** Plan only. Nothing here is implemented yet.
**Companion to:** `repositioning-audit.md` (Phase 2.2, 2.3) and `naming-terminology-map.md`.

## The framing (read this first)

This is a **medium-term** play, not the near-term revenue lever. Your own data (repositioning audit, Phase 2) says the site is a low-traffic, warm-audience validation tool: ~118 sessions a month, 74% direct, only ~8% organic. So proof and pricing clarity move revenue now; local SEO builds a pipeline of strangers over 3 to 9 months. Do it **alongside** the proof/pricing work, not instead of it, and don't expect fast movement.

**The angle that makes this worth doing:** don't fight for generic "Brisbane". Own **"web designer Moreton Bay / north Brisbane"** and your actual suburbs. It's far less contested, it matches where you are, and the demand already exists. Your GSC impressions show suburb and vertical queries you rank for but sit too low to be clicked (Caboolture, North Lakes, Sandgate, Geebung, Ipswich, plus "construction website design company" and "website designer for construction businesses"). You are not inventing demand, you are claiming demand you already appear for.

## Moves, in priority order

### 1. Google Business Profile (highest ROI, do first)
The single biggest local lever, and it's off-site so it doesn't touch the repo.
- Claim and fully verify the profile.
- Primary category: "Website designer". Add secondary categories (Marketing agency, Internet marketing service) as they fit.
- Set the **service area** to your real suburbs (Moreton Bay + north Brisbane), not a storefront radius, since you serve clients on-site rarely.
- Fill everything: services (mapped to your offerings and prices), description, hours, real photos of work and of you.
- Post weekly (a case study, an article, a tip). GBP posts are a ranking and freshness signal most solo operators ignore.
- Seed and maintain reviews (see move 6).

### 2. NAP consistency + LocalBusiness schema
- Decide your canonical **NAP** (Name, Address, Phone) and use it identically everywhere.
- The site already ships `ProfessionalService`/`LocalBusiness`-style JSON-LD (repositioning audit, Phase 1.5). Audit it and make sure it carries: consistent NAP, `areaServed` listing the target suburbs, and `geo` coordinates. This is a tighten, not a rebuild.
- Add the same NAP to the site footer in plain text if it isn't there.

### 3. Citations cleanup
- Fix the inconsistent citations flagged in the brief (Whereis, Tomedia, and any others) so NAP matches the canonical version everywhere. Inconsistent citations dilute local ranking signals.
- Get listed in a handful of quality AU directories with the exact NAP (True Local, Yellow, Hotfrog, local Chamber/BNI if you're a member).

### 4. On-page local signals (repo work)
- Put your **suburbs in plain text** on the homepage and the service/industry pages. Right now location lives mostly in meta and schema. Both Google and AI models read visible text first. (This is exactly what the sample visibility report preaches, so it's on-brand to practise it.)
- Add "Brisbane" and the region to key H1s/straplines where it reads naturally, without diluting the strong homepage H1 (add a location strap near it rather than jamming it in).
- Strengthen internal links: link `/web-development`, `/about`, and the industry pages to `/cost-of-a-website-in-brisbane` and to the location/service-area page below. `/cost-of-a-website-in-brisbane` already ranks (pos ~36 on 490 impressions) and just needs link equity and freshness, not a meta tweak.

### 5. A real service-area / location structure (repo work)
- Build one solid **"Web Design in Moreton Bay / North Brisbane"** service-area page (not doorway spam). Genuine content: the suburbs served, local context, local case studies, and a clear CTA.
- Only after that proves out, consider a small set of **suburb + service** pages targeting the exact high-intent queries already in GSC (e.g. "website design Caboolture", "web designer North Lakes"). Each must be genuinely distinct and useful, or it hurts more than it helps. Log any that are thin and cut them.

### 6. Reviews (compounds everything above)
- You have **5.0 from 11 Google reviews**. That's a real asset. Get it growing: a simple post-project ask, ideally prompting the client to mention what you did and roughly where they are.
- Surface the rating on-site (already added to the homepage proof band and the ProofCluster on service pages).

## Sequencing

1. **Now, in parallel with proof/pricing:** GBP (move 1), NAP + schema tighten (move 2), citations (move 3). These are mostly off-repo and low-risk.
2. **Next:** on-page local signals + internal links (move 4), the single service-area page (move 5 part one).
3. **Later, only if 1 to 2 show movement:** suburb pages (move 5 part two).
4. **Ongoing:** reviews (move 6), weekly GBP posts.

- The **suburbs/region** you actually want to serve and target (confirm Moreton Bay + which north-Brisbane suburbs).

## Measurement

- Track position for the target suburb + vertical queries in GSC (they're already appearing as impressions; the goal is to move them from page 3 to 6 up into clickable range).
- Watch the Google Business Profile insights (calls, direction requests, profile views) once it's live.
- Because this is warm-site + local, judge success on **local query positions and GBP actions**, not total sessions.
