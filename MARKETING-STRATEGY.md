# RAVENCI Solutions — Marketing Strategy

## Positioning

**Who we serve:** Established Australian businesses (primarily Brisbane and SE QLD) that need a website built properly — not a template, not a drag-and-drop page builder. Businesses that have outgrown their current site or been burned by a previous developer.

**What we do:** Custom web development, eCommerce platforms, branding, SEO, ongoing maintenance. Full lifecycle — design through to hosting and care.

**How we're different:** Structural engineer's approach. Built once, built properly, still working in five years. One person (Richard) owns the relationship. 25+ years, 450+ projects.

**Core message:** "I build websites that pay for themselves."

---

## Target Client Profile

- Revenue: $500K–$10M/year
- Industries: Professional services, healthcare, construction, retail/eCommerce
- Pain: Current site is broken, slow, template-based, or the previous developer disappeared
- Decision-maker: Business owner or operations manager
- Budget: $5K–$30K for a build, $250–$3K/month ongoing
- Trigger: Embarrassed by their current site, losing leads, or launching/rebranding

## Channel Strategy (Priority Order)

### 1. Google Business Profile (GBP) — Highest Priority

Your GBP listing is the single highest-leverage marketing asset for a local service business.

**Actions:**
- Fully optimise the GBP listing (services, description, photos, hours, Q&A)
- Post 1–2 updates per week (project completions, tips, behind-the-scenes)
- Respond to every review within 24 hours
- Add project photos regularly (showcase images work well here)
- Use the Q&A section to pre-answer common objections
- Ensure NAP (Name, Address, Phone) is consistent across all directories

**KPI:** Appear in the local 3-pack for "web developer Brisbane," "website design Brisbane," "ecommerce developer Brisbane"

### 2. Content & SEO — Compound Growth

You already have the article infrastructure. Use it.

**Content calendar (2 articles/month minimum):**
- Week 1: Educational article targeting a search query (e.g., "how much does a website cost in Brisbane," "Shopify vs custom website for Australian businesses")
- Week 3: Case study or project breakdown

**Article types that work for RAVENCI:**
- Cost guides (already have "cost of a website in Brisbane" — expand to eCommerce, branding)
- Comparison content ("WordPress vs Shopify vs Custom" — already have this, keep it fresh)
- Industry-specific guides ("medical practice website requirements" — already have this)
- "How to choose" guides ("how to choose a web designer in Brisbane" — already have this)
- Project breakdowns / lessons learned

**SEO priorities:**
- Continue building topical authority around "Brisbane web development" and "Australian eCommerce"
- Internal linking between articles, service pages, and case studies
- Keep existing content updated (quarterly refresh)
- Local citations and directory listings (Yellow Pages, True Local, Hotfrog, etc.)

### 3. LinkedIn — Professional Network

LinkedIn is where your target clients spend their professional time.

**Profile:**
- Position as "Brisbane Web Developer for Established Businesses" not "CEO at RAVENCI"
- Featured section: link to case studies, free audit tool, launch-your-vision page

**Content (2–3 posts/week):**
- Before/after website comparisons (visual posts perform well)
- Quick tips (accessibility, site speed, eCommerce conversion)
- Case study summaries with results
- Behind-the-scenes of builds (code screenshots, design decisions)
- Industry observations (what's changing in web, what businesses should know)

**Engagement:**
- Comment on posts by Brisbane business owners and industry contacts
- Join and participate in Brisbane business groups
- Connect with complementary professionals (accountants, marketers, business coaches)

**KPI:** 2–3 inbound enquiries per month from LinkedIn within 6 months

### 4. Referral Program — Lowest Cost Per Acquisition

Your best clients are your best salespeople.

**Structure:**
- Offer existing clients a referral incentive (e.g., 1 month free hosting, $250 credit, or a percentage of the referred project)
- Create a simple referral page or form
- Ask for referrals at natural touchpoints (project handover, 6-month check-in, annual review)
- Send a quarterly "who do you know?" email to active clients

**KPI:** 1–2 referred clients per quarter

### 5. Email Marketing (MailerLite) — Nurture Existing Leads

You have MailerLite set up with three groups. Use them.

**Flows:**
- **Visibility Check leads:** 3-email nurture sequence (deliver report → explain findings → offer free consultation)
- **Launch Your Vision leads:** Follow-up sequence if they don't convert immediately
- **Audit Report leads:** Educational sequence + soft CTA for a call

**Newsletter (monthly):**
- 1 useful tip or insight
- 1 recent project highlight
- 1 link to latest article
- Keep it short. Value-first, sell-second.

**KPI:** 25%+ open rate, 1–2 conversions per month from email

### 6. Google Ads — Supplement When Ready

Not the first priority, but valuable once organic foundations are solid.

**When to start:** After GBP is optimised and you're ranking organically for at least 5 target terms.

**Campaign types:**
- Search ads for high-intent queries ("web developer Brisbane," "ecommerce website Brisbane")
- Retargeting visitors who viewed service pages but didn't convert

**Budget:** Start at $500–$1,000/month, scale based on cost-per-lead
