## PART 1: Competitive Analysis & Market Position

### What You're Doing Well

1. **Transparent pricing** — Almost no Brisbane agency publishes pricing. Excite Media, Deepend, ROUNDHOUSE, Orange Digital — none of them show prices. You do. This is a genuine competitive weapon.
2. **Industry niche pages** (healthcare, construction) — Very few Brisbane agencies have dedicated vertical pages. Most are generalist. This builds topical authority for both SEO and AI search.
3. **Modern tech stack positioning** — No Brisbane competitor prominently positions around Next.js/React custom-coded performance. Competitors use WordPress/Webflow. This is a real differentiator.
4. **Clear process page** — The 6-step methodology with FAQ is excellent for trust-building.
5. **Retainer packages** — Smart recurring revenue model with clear tiering. The "vs hiring in-house" comparison is compelling.
6. **Founder-led story** — The structural engineer + graphic designer angle is genuinely unique and memorable.
7. **Lead magnet** (visibility check form) — Smart email capture mechanism.
8. **Development-only positioning** — This lane is essentially unoccupied in Brisbane.

### What to Improve

1. **Inconsistent stats across pages** — This is the most urgent credibility issue:
   - About page: "30+ Australian Businesses Served"
   - Web development page: "Trusted by 50+ businesses"
   - SEO page: "Trusted by 60+ Australian businesses"
   - Process page: "50+ Projects Delivered"
   - Pick ONE honest set of numbers and use them everywhere.

2. **Healthcare/Construction stat bars show words instead of numbers** — "Healthcare" with "Clients Served" underneath, and "Construction" with "Clients Served" — these look odd. Either show a real number or remove those cells.

3. **About page photo placeholder** — `src/app/about/page.tsx:97-108`: "A photo is coming soon, I promise" is actively hurting credibility. Research shows founder visibility is a top trust signal. Get a professional headshot or remove the placeholder entirely.

4. **No social media links anywhere** — No LinkedIn, Instagram, or Facebook. Even if you're not active, LinkedIn is expected for a B2B professional service.

5. **No Google reviews/ratings displayed** — Competitors like Chromatix prominently show "151+ five-star reviews." If you have Google reviews, surface them.

6. **Testimonials lack specificity** — No photos, no company names/logos, no specific outcomes. "Above and beyond" testimonials don't convert the $8K-$10K buyer. They need to see "RAVENCI rebuilt our site and enquiries doubled in 3 months."

7. **Case studies have no hard metrics** — Every `results.metrics` is empty. The results sections are vague paragraphs. Premium buyers need numbers: PageSpeed scores, load time improvements, lead generation numbers.

8. **No awards, certifications, or partnership badges** — WordPress partner, Vercel, Next.js — any ecosystem badges build credibility.

### What's Missing

1. Social media presence — LinkedIn at minimum for B2B
2. Google Business Profile integration — Critical for local SEO in Brisbane
3. Video testimonials — Highest trust format for agency sites
4. Comparison table — RAVENCI vs typical WordPress agency vs DIY builder
5. Client onboarding downloadable (PDF guide) — Good for high-ticket conversion
6. ABN/business registration — Expected for Australian businesses
7. Physical address — "Brisbane, Australia" is too vague; even a suburb helps local SEO
8. Accessibility statement page — You guarantee accessibility but have no statement
9. Cookie consent banner — You offer it as an add-on for clients but don't have one yourself
10. Custom 404 page — None found

### What to Remove or Change

1. The about page photo placeholder — Remove or replace immediately
2. "2032 Olympics Ready" stat on construction page — Feels forced and isn't a measurable metric
3. Commented-out Branding accordion on homepage (`page.tsx:185-188`) — Either restore it or clean it up
4. `font-poppins` class references — Referenced in testimonial sections but Poppins isn't loaded (you use Lexend)
5. The word "innovative" from meta description — Overused buzzword; replace with something concrete

## PART 2: Code Quality, Performance & UX/UI

### Bugs & Issues Found

| Issue | Location | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| Typo: "supproted" should be "supported" | `src/app/about/page.tsx:140` | Medium |
| `font-poppins` class used but font not loaded | `src/app/web-development/page.tsx:145`, `src/app/retainer-packages/page.tsx:192` | Low |
| OG image comment says "Replace with your image path once created" | `src/app/layout.tsx:44` | Medium — verify file exists |
| Quote page metadata says "Hosting & Maintenance" | `src/app/quote/page.tsx:5` | Low |
| Footer: Web App Dev, Mobile App Dev, SEO links are plain text (not `<Link>`) | `src/components/main-footer.tsx:53-65, 94-99` | Medium — dead navigation |
| Articles page uses `axios` instead of `fetch` | `src/app/articles/page.tsx:13` | Medium — loses Next.js caching/revalidation |
| Navigation `useEffect` missing `handleScroll` dependency | `src/components/main-navigation.tsx:120-127` | Low — potential stale closure |
| No `revalidate` or `cache` configuration on article fetches | `src/app/articles/page.tsx` | Medium — articles may be stale |

### Performance Concerns

1. **Two icon libraries loaded** — Both `@tabler/icons-react` and `lucide-react` are in dependencies. Each adds significant bundle size. Pick one. Tabler is the primary; remove Lucide unless shadcn/ui components need it.
2. **Scroll handler not throttled** — `src/components/main-navigation.tsx:105-117` fires `checkOverlappingElements` on every scroll event with DOM queries. Add `requestAnimationFrame` or throttle.
3. **All font weights loaded** — `src/app/layout.tsx:14-24` loads Lexend weights 100-900 and Playfair weights 400-900. Likely only 3-4 weights are used. Trim to reduce font payload.
4. **No `loading.tsx` or `error.tsx`** — No route-level loading states or error boundaries for any page. If Strapi goes down, the articles page will error with no fallback.
5. **Articles fetch has no error handling** — `src/app/articles/page.tsx:10-23`: If Strapi is down, the page crashes. Wrap in try/catch with a graceful fallback.
6. **No image dimensions on article featured images** — `src/app/articles/page.tsx:69-73` uses `backgroundImage` CSS instead of Next.js `<Image>` — loses all optimization (lazy loading, WebP, responsive sizing).

### UX/UI Issues

1. **Mobile hamburger menu visible alongside desktop nav** — The hamburger button is always rendered even on desktop where the full nav is visible. It should be `lg:hidden`.
2. **Choice paralysis on CTAs** — 4 different contact paths: "Launch Your Vision", "Get a Quote", "Visibility Check", "Footer Email Capture". Streamline to 2 max: primary CTA + lead magnet.
3. **No breadcrumbs** — On a 22+ page site, breadcrumbs help orientation and are excellent for SEO.
4. **Empty footer spacer sections** — Multiple pages end with empty `<section>` elements for spacing. Use padding/margin instead.
5. **PageSpeed guarantee inconsistency** — 80+ on the about page, 85+ on service pages, "95+" on the process page stats bar. Pick one number.

## PART 3: SEO, AEO & GEO Audit

### SEO — What's Working

- Sitemap dynamically generated with proper priorities
- robots.txt correctly configured
- JSON-LD structured data on most pages (ProfessionalService, FAQPage, AboutPage, CollectionPage)
- Page-level metadata on all pages
- OpenGraph and Twitter cards configured
- FAQ schema on service pages (boosts featured snippet potential)
- `en_AU` locale set
- Clean URL structure

### SEO — Critical Gaps

1. **No `LocalBusiness` schema** — Only has `ProfessionalService`. For Brisbane-focused local SEO, add `LocalBusiness` with `geo`, `latitude`, `longitude`, `openingHours`, `aggregateRating`.
2. **No `Service` schema** — Individual service pages (web-development, healthcare, construction) should each have `Service` schema with `provider`, `serviceType`, `areaServed`, `offers`.
3. **No `Review`/`AggregateRating` schema** — Testimonials exist but aren't marked up for rich results. Massive missed opportunity for star ratings in search results.
4. **No `Article`/`BlogPosting` schema** — Articles from Strapi have no structured data at all.
5. **No `BreadcrumbList` schema** — Missing breadcrumbs both visually and in markup.
6. **No geo meta tags** — Missing `<meta name="geo.region" content="AU-QLD">` and `<meta name="geo.placename" content="Brisbane">`.
7. **No canonical tags explicitly set** — While Next.js handles this by default, explicit canonicals prevent duplicate content issues.
8. **Homepage inherits generic metadata** — No page-specific metadata for the homepage. It should have its own optimized title/description.
9. **Missing `hreflang`** — Even a basic `<link rel="alternate" hreflang="en-au">` signals geographic intent.
10. **Articles bypass Next.js caching** — Using `axios` instead of `fetch` means Strapi content isn't cached/revalidated by Next.js ISR. Use `fetch` with `{ next: { revalidate: 3600 } }`.

### AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) — Gaps

AI search engines (Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, ChatGPT with browsing) now drive 240% more AI-referral traffic year-over-year, and that traffic converts at **14.2% vs 2.8%** for traditional organic.

1. **Content isn't "answer-first"** — Page copy buries key information in flowing paragraphs. AI engines cite content that answers queries in the opening 40-60 words. Each service page should open with a concise, self-contained answer paragraph.
2. **FAQ answers are too long** — The sweet spot for AI citation is 30-50 word answers. Current FAQ answers average 60-100+ words. Consider shorter primary answers with expandable detail.
3. **No comparison content** — Pages that compare options (e.g., "WordPress vs Custom Next.js", "Template vs Custom Website") are heavily cited by AI engines.
4. **No "People Also Ask" targeting** — Create content addressing: "How much does a website cost in Brisbane?", "Best web developer in Brisbane", "How long does it take to build a website?"

### GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) — Gaps

1. **No original research or proprietary data** — AI engines need a reason to cite YOUR content specifically. Publish your own data: "We audited 50 Brisbane business websites — here's what we found." This is the most powerful GEO play available.
2. **Low entity density** — Pages with 15+ recognized entities show 4.8x higher AI citation probability. Content mentions few specific tools, frameworks, or industry terms by name.
3. **No third-party validation signals** — PR, guest posts, industry directory listings, and earned media are critical for LLMs to decide which sources to cite.
4. **No cross-platform presence** — Ensure RAVENCI appears consistently across: Google Business Profile, LinkedIn, Clutch, GoodFirms, DesignRush, ProductHunt, and industry directories.

## PART 4: Pricing Validation

### Is $8,000-$10,000 fair for development-only in Brisbane?

**Yes — and potentially slightly undercharging for custom Next.js work.**

Market context (AUD, 2026):

| Website Type | Price Range |
|---|---|
| Basic template/DIY site | $1,000 - $5,000 |
| Standard business site (design + dev) | $5,000 - $10,000 |
| Advanced small business (10-15 pages) | $10,000 - $15,000 |
| Small eCommerce (WooCommerce/Shopify) | $9,000 - $15,000 |
| Custom web applications | $40,000 - $100,000+ |

| Provider Type | Hourly Rate |
|---|---|
| WordPress freelancer average | $40 - $75 |
| Mid-level freelance developer | $80 - $120 |
| Senior/expert freelancer | $120 - $150+ |
| Small agency | $100 - $180 |
| Large/enterprise agency | $180 - $250+ |

**Key comparisons:**
- Chillybin (Brisbane WordPress specialist) starts at $6,000 for WordPress
- A Next.js specialist at $120-$180/hr for 55-80 hours = $6,600-$14,400
- Full-service agency builds: $15,000-$25,000+

Current Growth Website at $7,490 (up to 8 pages, CMS, SEO, analytics) is competitive for custom Next.js. For development-only (client supplies design), $8,000-$10,000 is well justified given custom-coded Next.js, sub-2-second load times, SEO-ready architecture, performance guarantees, and ongoing support.

### To attract 2-3 clients at $8K-$10K

The biggest gap isn't pricing — it's **proof**. Case studies need hard metrics. The $8K-$10K buyer does due diligence. They'll compare case studies against agencies showing "300% traffic increase" or "2.1s load time down to 0.8s." Current results sections say things like "transformed their digital presence" — that doesn't close a $10K deal.
